Key Insights

3 Things That Made Ligety Untouchable

⚡
Fact 01

Extreme Hip Angulation & Edge Angles

Ligety achieved edge angles exceeding 70°. By aggressively projecting his hips into the hill while keeping his upper body calm and facing downhill, he carved arcs at speeds that would send most skiers sliding.

Biomechanics
🎯
Fact 02

Early Turn Initiation & "Blowing the Gate"

Ligety started his turns so early he appeared to ski directly into gates. This early pressure into the top of the arc generates speed through the fall line rather than losing it — exiting each gate faster than anyone else.

Tactics
🏔️
Fact 03

Crossover vs. Cross-Under Transition

Instead of pulling his feet under his body (cross-under), Ligety drove his body over the skis (crossover), arriving at the new edge while already angulated. This eliminates the flat-ski moment entirely.

Technique

Practical Application

How to Apply Ligety's Principles

📐

Practice "hip-to-the-hill" drills

Push your inside hip aggressively toward the slope surface mid-turn. Use a pole touch to stay patient and balanced.

⏱️

Initiate turns earlier than feels natural

Commit to starting your edge change before you think you need to. Early engagement = clean arc. Late initiation = skidded recovery.

🔁

Drive your body over the skis

Let your center of mass fall forward and across. Think "falling into the next turn" rather than stepping into it.

🎿

Keep your upper body quiet & downhill

Angulation happens from the hip down. Shoulders remain level and pointing downhill — separation between upper and lower body is key.

📹

Video yourself and compare

Record your own runs. The most common gap: your turn entry is too late and your hips are too upright.

💪

Build lateral hip strength off-snow

Side planks, lateral band walks, and single-leg squats are direct carryovers to the angulation demands of Ligety's technique.
